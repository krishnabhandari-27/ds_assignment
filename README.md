Trader Performance vs Market Sentiment Analysis
Overview

This project analyzes the relationship between Bitcoin market sentiment (Fear/Greed Index) and trader behavior on Hyperliquid. The objective is to understand how sentiment impacts trading performance, activity levels, and directional bias, and to derive actionable strategy insights.

Methodology

Loaded and cleaned trader execution data and sentiment data.

Converted timestamps to daily level and merged datasets by date.

Created key metrics:

Daily total PnL

Win rate

Trade frequency

Average trade size

Long/Short ratio

Compared performance across Fear vs Greed regimes.

Built trader segments:

Frequent vs Infrequent traders

Consistent winners vs Inconsistent traders

Analyzed performance and behavior differences across segments.

Key Insights

Performance is higher during Fear periods.
Average daily PnL is significantly higher during Fear and Extreme Fear days, though risk and price swings are also elevated.

Traders become more active during Fear.
Trade frequency increases sharply during Fear regimes, indicating stronger participation during uncertain market conditions.

Directional bias shifts with sentiment.
Traders take more long positions during Fear and relatively more short positions during Greed, suggesting sentiment-driven positioning behavior.

Strategy Recommendations

1. Risk-Adjusted Trading During Fear
Increase participation during Fear regimes but reduce position size to manage higher downside risk.

2. Sentiment-Based Directional Bias
Favor long setups during Fear regimes and reduce long exposure or consider short positions during Greed regimes.
